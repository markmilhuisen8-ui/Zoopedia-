Animal Vault

Predatores

Lion

The mighty lion lives in large social groups called prides. Adult males grow thick manes and possess a loud roar. These powerful cats hunt big prey across the grassy plains.

View

Tiger

The orange tiger has dark stripes and lives alone mostly. These massive cats are very strong and love to swim. They hunt deep inside the thick forests of the East.

View

Wolf

The grey wolf lives and hunts in a smart pack. They howl loudly to talk with others from far away. These wild dogs can survive in the very cold snow.

View

Crocodile

The green crocodile lives in the water and the land. They have very strong jaws and a long scaly tail. These old reptiles hide and wait for their next meal.

View

Cheetah

The fast cheetah runs very quickly to catch its prey. They have black spots and a very lean long body. These thin cats hunt during the day in open fields.

View

Owl

The owl flies very quietly through the dark night sky. They have big eyes and can hear a tiny mouse. These birds can turn their heads almost all the way.

View

Hyena

The spotted hyena lives in a large and loud clan. They make a sound that feels like a human laugh. These smart animals hunt well and eat every single bone.

View

Eagle

The big eagle flies high and has very sharp eyes. They use strong claws to grab fish from the lake. These birds build large nests on the very high rocks.

View

Brown Bear

The brown bear eats fish and berries in the woods. They are very big and sleep through the cold winter. These fuzzy giants use sharp claws to dig for food.

View

Snow Leopard

The snow leopard lives high up in the cold mountains. Their thick grey fur keeps them warm in the frost. These cats are very hard to find in the rocks.

Primary

Forest Leopard

The forest leopard climbs up tall trees with great ease. They hide their food high up away from other beasts. Their spotted coat blends in with the dark green leaves.

View

Polar Bear

The polar bear is the largest land carnivore and is perfectly adapted to the Arctic. Their white fur and thick layer of blubber protect them from freezing temperatures.
