Wild Animals

Elephant

Large grey trunks spray cool water over dusty skin. Heavy feet stomp through the golden savanna.

View

Giraffe

Long patterned necks reach toward the highest leaves. Spotted bodies glide across the vast open plains.

View

Zebra

Bold black stripes cover their powerful running bodies. Shaggy manes flicker under the hot midday sun.

View

Panda

Soft white fur meets black patches around their eyes. Quiet giants chew on green stalks today.

View

Deer

Deer are graceful herbivorous mammals with slender bodies and antlers. Their speed and alert nature help them escape from predators.

View

Monkey

Monkeys are intelligent primates that are highly social and live in groups called troops. Their ability to climb trees and use tools makes them very adaptable.

View

Kangaroo

Kangaroos are marsupials native to Australia known for their hopping movement and pouches. Their powerful tails help them balance while jumping.

View

Rhinoceros

Rhinoceroses are large mammals with thick skin and one or two horns. Despite their size, they can run very fast and are highly protective.

View

Hippopotamus

Hippopotamuses are large semi-aquatic animals that spend most of their time in water. Despite a calm appearance, they can be very aggressive.

View

Fox

Foxes are small to medium-sized mammals known for their cunning nature and sharp senses. Their bushy tails help them balance and keep warm.

View

Bison

Bison are large mammals with massive heads, thick fur, and strong bodies. They live in herds and play an important role in maintaining ecosystems.

View

Vulture

Broad wings circle high above the dusty desert floor. Sharp eyes watch for movement from the sky.
