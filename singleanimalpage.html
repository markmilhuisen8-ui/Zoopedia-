Red Panda

Species Overview

The Red Panda, often described as a living fossil, is a master of the high-altitude forest canopy. Despite its name, it is more closely related to weasels and raccoons than to giant pandas. These solitary creatures spend most of their lives in trees, using their long, bushy tails for balance and as a wraparound blanket during chilly Himalayan nights. Their diet consists primarily of bamboo, though they are known to snack on fruits, acorns, and the occasional bird egg.
